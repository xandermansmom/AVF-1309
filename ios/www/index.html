Project Intent

Perks

Less obvious perks of using 'live' data resources include logins and payments. How could Twitter be used in these two scenarios?

Some perks of using Twitter login with your app is explained in Twitter’s developer documentation at https://dev.twitter.com/docs/auth/sign-twitter.

Logins

  • Ease of use - A new visitor to your site only has to click two buttons in order to sign in for the first time.
  • Twitter integration - The Sign in with Twitter flow can grant authorization to use Twitter APIs on your users behalf.
  • OAuth based (Open Authorization) - A wealth of client libraries and example code are compatible with Sign in with Twitter's API.

Payments

An example of how Twitter is already being used for payments is a startup called Chirpify.

Chirpify allows a brand with a following on Twitter to connect its PayPal business account to them and then tweet items for sale through a simple dashboard. This could include time-sensitive deals or highlighted items that are on sale or need to be moved. When the user replies with a tweet saying they want to buy the product, the money gets deducted from their PayPal account using OAuth permission. If the buyer doesn’t have a PayPal account, they are prompted to set one up and connect it to Chirpify.

Why would we need JSON-P, specifically? JSONP is important for developers who want to access data that exists outside the website the user is visiting. Since that is what API’s do, access data outside the website, this makes JSONP important to us as developers.

Briefly describe some past 'lessons learned' regarding workflow as it relates to research, design, development, etc. First and foremost, when it comes to workflow, a lesson I learned early on was taking micro breaks in between coding. Spending all day and evening working on your code sometimes stifles your creative flow and oftentimes causes unnecessary coding errors due to staring at the same code all day.

As far as research is concerned, I learned the trusted resources to search for answers. One of my favorites is StackOverflow and of course, the FSO Facebook group when I have trouble finding answers to my questions.

When it comes to design, I’m engaged to a graphic designer with 18 years experience in the profession and he has taught me that in order to be successful, you create multiple images very quickly and settle on the best, never second guessing your choice because in the past, I have spent countless hours working and creating images and graphics for my app, only to second guess myself and start over. This process would put me behind deadline so I have learned to take his advice when it comes to graphics and design.

You should have a clear idea where you are going with your app so jotting down criteria for your app and researching how to meet that criteria before you actually start developing your app is beneficial.
